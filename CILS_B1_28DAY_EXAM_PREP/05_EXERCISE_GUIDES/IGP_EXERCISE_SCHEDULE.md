## ‚ö†Ô∏è COMMON MISTAKES TO WATCH FOR

### Chapter 13 (Direct Pronouns):
- ‚ùå Using "lo" for feminine nouns ("la torta" ‚Üí "lo" is WRONG, should be "la")
- ‚ùå Forgetting past participle agreement ("le ho mangiate" NOT "le ho mangiato")
- ‚ùå Apostrophe errors ("l'ho visto" YES, but "li ho visti" NO apostrophe)

### Chapter 14 (Prepositions):
- ‚ùå "Vado a Italia" (WRONG ‚Üí "Vado in Italia")
- ‚ùå "Abito in via Garibaldi" (WRONG ‚Üí "Abito in via Garibaldi" is CORRECT, but "vivo a Roma" needs "a")
- ‚ùå Articulated preposition errors ("della casa" vs. "di casa" - depends on article!)

### Chapter 15 (Indirect Pronouns):
- ‚ùå Using "loro" instead of "gli" for plural ("Do il libro a loro" ‚Üí informal, use "gli do il libro")
- ‚ùå Confusing "gli" (to him) and "le" (to her)
- ‚ùå Double object pronoun order: "glielo" NOT "loglie"

### Chapter 20 (Ci and Ne):
- ‚ùå Using "ci" with quantities (should be "ne": "ne prendo due" NOT "ci prendo due")
- ‚ùå Using "ne" for places (should be "ci": "ci vado domani" NOT "ne vado domani")
- ‚ùå Agreement with past participle: "ne ho comprate tre" (agrees with "tre [torte]")
