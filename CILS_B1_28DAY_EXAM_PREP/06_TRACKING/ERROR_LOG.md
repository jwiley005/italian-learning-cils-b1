**Purpose**: Identify recurring error PATTERNS (not one-off mistakes)

## üìù HOW TO USE THIS LOG

### When to Log Errors:
- After each grammar exercise (cloze tests)
- After each writing practice email
- After each speaking practice (self-assessment)
- After each listening comprehension exercise
- After each reading comprehension passage
- **ESPECIALLY after mock exams** (critical data)

### What to Log:
1. **Date**: When the error occurred
2. **Section**: Grammar, Writing, Speaking, Listening, Reading
3. **Error Made**: What you said/wrote incorrectly
4. **Correction**: The correct form
5. **Category**: Type of error (see categories below)
6. **Frequency**: How many times you've made this error (1st, 2nd, 3rd+)
7. **Action**: What you'll do to fix it (review cards, drill exercise, etc.)

### Error Categories:
- **Prepositions** (di, a, da, in, su, con + articles)
- **Pronouns** (direct/indirect: lo, la, gli, le, mi, ti, ci, vi)
- **Ci/Ne** (locative/partitive particles)
- **Verb Conjugations** (present, past, future tense)
- **Articles** (un, una, uno, il, la, gli, etc.)
- **Gender Agreement** (adjective endings: -o/-a/-i/-e)
- **Vocabulary** (wrong word choice)
- **Word Order** (syntax errors)
- **Spelling** (orthography)
- **Listening Comprehension** (missed key information)

## üí° TIPS FOR EFFECTIVE ERROR LOGGING

1. **Be Specific**: Don't just write "grammar error" - specify the exact rule
2. **Track Frequency**: This is the most important data point
3. **Review Weekly**: Every Sunday, analyze patterns from the week
4. **Prioritize 3+ Frequency Errors**: These are your blind spots
5. **Don't Log One-Off Mistakes**: Focus on patterns, not flukes
6. **Use This Before Mock Exams**: Review your error log before Days 17, 20, 23
7. **Update Anki**: If you find a gap, create a new Anki card for it
